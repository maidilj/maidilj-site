About

I'm a final-year Information and Communications Technology student at Singapore Institute of Technology, specialising in artificial intelligence. I work at the intersection of machine learning and software engineering — building models that find structure in messy real-world data and applications that put that structure to use.

My most technically demanding work has been in clinical AI: implementing deep learning architectures for clustering multivariate patient trajectories with missing values — the kind of incomplete, irregular data that real clinical databases actually contain. On the applied side, I've built full-stack web applications designed to connect people with social services. Both tracks share the same conviction: the best technical work reduces real friction for real people.

I'm actively looking for roles in AI/ML engineering, data science, or software engineering where I can keep learning from hard problems.
